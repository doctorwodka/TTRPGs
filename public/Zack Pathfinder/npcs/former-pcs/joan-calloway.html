Character Questions

  • Why is your character willing to go to the thawing mechanical lands?

    • She's tasked with making sure Caoilinn O'Connor doesn't flake out on her debts. Where Caoilinn goes, she goes. Any gold is good gold, and since much of the gold she had has gone to her family.

  • What do they wish to find there?

    • Ideally? A way to drag her family up from the hell they've been tossed in. Realistically? Something that she can prove her worth to those who love her.

  • Where are they from?

    • Thernos. Her family still lives there, though their home is not as nice as it once was.

  • Who were they to the community they were from?

    • Second child of the former noble Calloway family, as well as the family disappointment; she was sent off to fight and couldn't even do that right.

  • Who is their closest person? Where is that person right now? (Bonus points for 2-3 more people)

    • Cyril Calloway - Her elder brother and the "golden child" of the family. He's back at home trying to make connections with the newly risen nobility in the hopes he can pull enough strings and get their family back on their feet. He is who she sends her earned gold off to, since she doesn't particularly trust their other family members.
    • Ansgar Nightstone - An older dwarven man from Hols who pitied Joan enough after the dissolution of the old nobility to hand her a couple "freebie" jobs. He typically sends her jobs with the promise of getting a cut of her reward in turn.

  • What’s the most impactful moment in your characters life so far? (Bonus points for 2-3 more key moments)

    • The first time she realized she was different from her family.
      "Joan," her brother lamented, bandaging her upper arm. He was twelve, and she was ten. "You can't keep doing this. Mother will throw a fit if she sees you've gotten hurt again."

      "But-" she starts, and is quickly interrupted.

      "No buts. You're a Calloway, not a street rat. And you haven't attended studies in nearly three days."

      She hated her studies. They all knew it. It seemed, too, that she was the only one who hated their studies. Why wouldn't she, when all they learnt about half the time were nobles who leered at her for her strange eyes, for her blunt attitude.

      She hated them, and she suspected they hated her.

      "...Yes, Cyril," murmured Joan for not the first time in her life, but it was likely the most bitter one spoken so far.

    • The first time she got into a fight.
      She's fifteen. Her blood had felt like it was on fire, and she couldn't help but grin. She's sure she looked demented, as blood leaked from her broken nose and she took another swing.

      "The hell's wrong with her?!" one of the men shouted. He'd dropped the gold he stole long ago, and his buddies seemed to be trying to pick it up while they fought. She laughed, which only seemed to weird him out more.

      "Shit's not right!" He managed to kick her, sending her halfway acrosd the alley and leaving her sprawled on the hard ground. He and his buddies took that as their chance to book it.

      She got up still grinning that bloody grin.

    • The first time she was actually thanked for helping.
      She's twenty-three.

      "Thank you," the child had murmured through tears as Joan set her down. Both their clothes were a bit dirtied, but they were otherwise unharmed. Her own new dress, however, was ruined, with a large slice through the front.

      Joan blinked, almost alarmed as her heart jackrabbited in her chest. She'd just stopped some asshole creep from hurting the child, but this... was new. She couldn't ever remember the last time she was thanked for something.

      "It's... not problem, dear," Joan managed out, but her smile was intense. It didn't even go away after she got home and got her scolding.

  • What’s your characters goal?

    • Gathering up enough gold and influence to make sure her family back home is safe, happy, and taken care of, no matter what it takes.

  • What do you, as a player, want to get out of playing this character narratively?

    • I'd like her to realize that she's worth more than her ability to make money or fight. She's currently pretty single minded on that, but I think being in a party will help fix her of that habit.

  • What do you, as a player, want to get out of this character from a mechanical/gameplay perspective?

    • i love playing a character that can hit hard! i havent played a fighter yet, so i'll likely be getting a feel of the play style as the game progresses

  • From a story perspective, why is your character the class they are? Why aren’t they any other class?

    • She's never been smart or charismatic enough like her brother to be in high society, and the fact of the matter is, she's been picking fights since she was a teenager.

  • How do your highest skills reflect your characters personality?

    • Due to not holding up to expectations had of her, she never truly held onto the majority of things she was taught and instead did her own thing growing up.
    • (Athletics +7) She was always more interested building herself up than learning about the noble families she was supposed to know (Society +3). This led to her sneaking out as a child often (Stealth +5).
    • As of late, she's been given a good couple "hunt this person down" jobs, so she's been learning how to deal with the wilderness and follow tracks (Survival +4).

  • Describe your characters moral or ethical beliefs. What would have to happen for them to go against their morals?

    • Be altruistic.

    • You have to do whatever it takes to survive.

    • Don't lie. Everyone lies, but you don't have to.

    • Pay a fair price... and if others don't, make sure they do.

    • Her trust has to be lost. Betrayal is not something she takes lightly, especially when it affects those she cares about.

  • How is the relationship with her parents? Are they alive or did they die during the rebellion?

    • It was strained while growing up, and still remains strained. Her father was injured during the rebellion and has been bed ridden since. Her mother stays by his side. She still sometimes writes her own letter and sends it along with Cyril's, but it's grown less frequent than it used to be.

  • What assets does her brother control, are they even enough to still consider the family a noble house?

    • The majority of their land was seized during the rebellion, alongside their old family home. They managed to keep some of their land and built a relatively nice home, but they would certainly no longer be considered a noble house. They have no authority in the area anymore, but Cyril is still trying to pull some strings to see if that could change.

  • How would you describe her fighting style? Is it more fencing based or street fighting based?

    • A mixture of both, but she leans more heavily into the street fighting as of late with there being no one to train with her like she had as a child. She's more of a heavy hitter as of late, but still tries to stay light on her feet in case things go south.

  • What type of fighting did she do during the rebellion? Why was she considered a failure there?

    • She wasn't able to protect her father like she intended to. She got too wrapped up in making sure that insurgents didn't get past her that she wasn't able stop her father from getting hurt. And, when he did, she abandoned her spot to keep his attacker from dealing the finishing blow. She wasn't the first to abandon their spot, or get cut down, but the way her father blamed her felt like she was.
