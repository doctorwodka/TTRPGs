Character Info

Ended up raising Viktor as his own kid, but didn't hide that he wasn't Viktor's bio dad. Wasn't the closest with the kids since he was too engrossed with writing, so he's not that knowledgeable with what they're actually Like, but he tried on occasion

    Character questions
  • Who are you now?
    • Viktor, human? A medic, mostly, but can hurt when cornered. 40-ish.- Why are you the class you are? Why not another class?
    • Being a vampire requires a certain bit of stealth, a bit of a silver-tongue, just to get out of situations he hadn't wanted to be in. He'd never been a very religious person, nor did he have the strength to haul someone out of the way, but he had a nimble touch, and over the years, he was forced to hone it into something like this.
  • What do you specialize in?
    • Medicine, mostly. He was handy with a scalpel in life, and he still practices where he can in the modern day.
  • Describe your characters personal edicts and anathemas. Come up with a situation where they would to go against these beliefs. These may change as the adventure goes on, we just need a starting off point.
    • Master the Art of Escape: Always have multiple escape routes planned; a successful exit is as important as a successful entry.
    • Do no harm. Pretty self-explanatory. Of course, it only extends as long as he's not the one being harmed, or he's taking a risk.
    • Never abandon the injured in pursuit of personal gain.
  • What are your characters goals? What do they wish to see in the world?
    • Help more people than he harms. Make amends with the people he left back at home. He wants to be
  • How did you wind up in Ensile, in a small town where the mountains meet the river?
    • Traveling from town to town, grove to grove, under the cover of night, mostly. It's pure happenstance that he ended up here
  • How did you get your abilities/class? Were you formally trained/a weird by product of undeath/gifted natural?
    • He wasn't very good at it in the beginning, but overtime has gotten quite skilled in slinking into the shadows. He regrets those early days, where he didn't know how to control his hunger and he'd gotten caught one too many times by too nosy of people.
  • Who were you when you were alive?
    • Dr Viktor Keson. He had his vices, like the occasional drink before bed, or making bets that he couldn't hold up half the time, but he wasn't a bad man. Or, at least, he didn't think he was. Was 33 when he died.
  • What did you do while you were alive?
    • He was a small town doctor; he took care of the people in his home as best he could, and he was pretty good at what he did.
  • What is your strongest memory from before then? How many memories did you retain?
    • Patching up a trio of children who'd gotten a bit too brave and tried to scare off a wild creature that'd gotten a bit too close to town. It had been a long night that night, with kids and family crying, but they'd survived. Long enough to hear their parents chew them out for scaring them half to death, at least.
  • What would you have been if you didn't die?
    • Probably still that small town doctor. Might've settled down, had a family. Just a normal guy living a normal life.
  • Where were you from?
    • Town in southern Tunseel (Frontillé in Vitry-Seilave) (happy to build this w/ you or do some little building of it myself so u don't have to think That hard abt it). Not even much of a town; it'd be more accurate to call it a village. Not even somewhere that notable, just another stop on the way to the capital.
  • Who were you closest to when you were alive?
    • His half-sister, Sabine Keson.
  • Who did you consider your family?
    • The people who came and checked in on him throughout the day at work. It takes a village to raise a person, and he felt sufficiently like family to (mostly) everyone at home. Old Isobel Rainier, though, who took in the kids so their mothers could work without worrying, was basically a mother figure to him.
  • How did you die?
    • It was a patient, brought in because some people found her on the edge of town, unconscious and visibly injured. He thought, at first, that she was dead with how shallowly she breathed, but he quickly found out that it was a ploy. When his back was turned, she attacked, draining him of his blood until he died.
  • If possible for the character give 3 NPCs you knew while alive. Do they know your current condition?
    • Sabine Keson - His half-elven half-sister who was the one who found him after his death. He only found this out because she was also at his grave when he had to drag himself out of it. He was partially delirious, half convinced he was dreaming. He can hardly remember how he got the blood on his hands when he fully came back to himself. He doesn't think he wants to know if she survived. Long, red-brown hair that reaches about mid-back. Appeared young, but wasn't more than 4-5 year younger than him. Her fingers were always callused from working the loom.
    • Demetric Beckete - One of his patients; they seemed to show up at least once every other week to make sure he checked out whatever injury they'd just sustained. They seemed to like the care and attention he gave. Gnome with vibrant green hair, a wirey build, and as nervous as they came.
    • Olivier Comtois - The town drunk. He'd gotten into an altercation with him once or twice, when he wouldn't let the guy boss him around. They'd both ended up with black eyes that day, and it was quite embarrassing for both of them. Didn't stop them from getting into another fight later on. Human, light brown hair, with a farmer's tan.

  • How do you feel about your undeath?
    • It's a curse..... bleh bleh bleh..... no, but externally he may express dislike of it, internally he's almost relieved after all these years; he would have lived and died in that town had he lived, and being able to get away is almost a blessing in disguise. He's seen more of the world than he'd ever had.
  • Do you know who turned you into that type of undead? What's your relationship with them?
    • He didn't know her at first, but over the years, he found out more and more information about her. Her name, or at least the name she was currently using, was Dione; she'd been doing what she did to him for several months at that point to people throughout the region. Only a few of those sired by her had survived, but no one was pleased with her tactics. He has yet to meet her again, but he's not too sure he'd be too happy to deal with her again.
  • Why did you decide to continue with your undeath instead of accepting final death?
    • He considered it at first, but decided against it. He still had people to help, even if he wasn't all that alive himself. That made him more objective, more determined to power through it all.
  • Do those that knew you when you were alive know about your death/undeath?
    • They certainly know about his death. Undeath is a harder question to answer; his sister, yes, if she's alive. If anyone else does, he'd be more surprised no one had hunted him down sooner.
  • How have you been 'sustaining' yourself?
    • A mixture of blood from people who tried to hurt him and those who willingly let him feed. The first is more common than he would like. The occasional animal should he need to feed, but he never kills the animal if he can help it.

    • Anra - Elven thaumaturge who sent him running after being called to the town he was in. Seems like word got to her that there was a vampire problem that needed to be solved. He thinks she lost his scent once he got far enough away, but he's still keeping an eye out for bright blonde curls and the scowl she makes.

  • What was your life like before you became undead?
    • "Ah. Well. Back in Frontillé, my parents were, ah, busy growing up. My mother worked, my father was in his study most of the time. My sister, Sabine, she, er, helped me study when I became interested in medicine. It.. wasn't easy, but she was there with me. I took over the clinic when Dr. Émile retired and things were... busy, but good. I helped people. I still try, where I can."
  • How did you come to be undead? Was it by choice, accident, or something else?
    • "An accident, I... suppose? I'm still coming to terms with it."
  • What challenges do you face as an undead individual in society?
    • "The distrust is the most alarming. Being pinned the blame for crimes is another. I try to stay to myself, but fingers still get pointed in my direction."
  • What misconceptions about the undead would you like to address?
    • "We're not... bloodthirsty killers, as it were. Nor did we all choose to become as we are. It's more common than one would expect."
  • How did you come to become familiar with the king?
    • "Ah. He, er, paid the funeral parlor a visit, we had a discussion. You can imagine it was, ah, a bit of a surprise that he invited us to come visit Tunseel. How could I say no?"
  • Do you feel emotions the same way you did when you were alive?
    • "I... would think so? I'm not that old. Though Yurei does make me feel that way sometimes. My people skills haven't fared well but- my emotions are still strong."
  • Can you describe what your existence feels like—physically, mentally, or spiritually?
    • "That's, ah, a weighted question. I'm- cold? You take the sun for granted until you can't see it. It's a bit, er, draining, I suppose, to think about what the ones you care about are up to. If they're okay and... all that. Lonely, I suppose. Er. Haha."
  • What’s your relationship with the living? Do you have living friends or family?
    • "I'm still a doctor. I do still want to help people. They're the ones who've grown a bit more, ah, distrustful of me, as it were."
  • Do you see your undead state as a curse or an opportunity? Why?
    • "It's... both, in a way? I'm not the same as I was before, but I wouldn't have been able to travel like I do now. I've seen- beautiful things, grotesque things, things that have confused me. Being like I am now- it's not what I wanted, but it's made me reconsider things."
  • What rights or protections do you think undead individuals should have?
    • "Well. Not being attacked for simply passing through town would be.. nice. Er. A hard thing to ask for, I know, haha. Perhaps districts where we might be able to be without worry?"
  • Are you part of a larger undead community or organization?
    • "Er. Not that I am aware of? I have not spent much time around other undead beyond those I travel with."
  • Do you have pets? If so, are they undead too?
    • "Ah. No. I like animals as much as the next person but they, ah, do not particularly like me. Not that I haven't tried, of course. But it is, ah, a bit hard to grow attached nowadays."
  • What’s your ultimate goal as an undead—eternal existence, redemption, or something else?
    • "I don't know. I have more time in the world now than I ever really considered, so it's... hm. Looking for forgiveness from those I've... unintentionally hurt would be ideal. Baby steps, for now."

    • "Er. Not particularly. Am I supposed to advertise something here?"
