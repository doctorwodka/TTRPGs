Fitzie Fairntickle

Info


Gnomish wizard from Collegium Arcanum in Caldrith looking for her missing wife Lila Fairntickle

Melissa

SPOILERS

The city itself appears stressed. The most powerful mages seem to spend most of their days casting walls of stone in order to expand the walls and ports rather than doing research leaving the students to run amok. The lesser fey of grimples and miflits, or of brownies and toothfairies stay far away from the Arcadum preferring the softer targets that won’t banish them. You make most of your living off of helping people with these pests though you’ve yet to have to encounter a major fey and gods willing it’ll stay that way.

  • Your wife went missing around the time the old Duke Miryel Siroth went missing. She worked as an aid to him occasionally, and he’d seek her out over Ephiane Savile for some reason you were not privy to.
  • Thoqiohne has a fondness for guns. A fellow student, Sifri Emberbead (Kobold Witch), has started talking to him about it. Though he has never been spotted firing one.
  • You’re fairly certain your acquaintance Thromulus was not responsible, he lacks the magic necessary and has his own missing person from around that same time.
  • During the time when the stars were gone, only 51 stars have come back to the night sky. They are not the same ones as before.

It was the same day that Lila went missing.

Background & History

  • Who were you before the campaign?
    • On the path of fully solidifying her thesis and graduating from her school. Potentially was going to become a fully blown adventurer like her mother, maybe even join her.
  • Are you tied to any orders, kingdoms, faiths, or circles?
    • She’s a student at the Collegeum Arcanum, but otherwise no.
  • What was your life before you became part of that group or cause?
    • Bored out of her mind and directionless. It took the beginnings of gray hair at a young age that scared her mom and Aldri into enrolling her at the school, whereupon she brighted up almost immediately after.
  • What is your strongest memory from earlier in life? Are there memories you’ve lost or ones that haunt you?
    • She remembers when her mom and her sibling got into a big argument regarding their parents. Fitzie remembers standing outside the door, wanting to show them the book that she had picked up with Aldri that afternoon. Fennel had stormed off, all but accidentally ramming into Fitzie in the process. She remembers how they had quietly apologized and brought her to her feet. They had seemed so sad, but even after being there for the whole conversation, Fitzie can’t remember what started the argument.
  • If your life had gone differently, what would you be doing now?
    • Likely still studying underneath professor Ildor Ilnudor, maybe further helping him with his research into the stars post-Rozen Crisis. If she could, she’d probably be joining her mom on adventures.
  • Where were you born or raised?
    • A small town in western Emerald Coast (name tbd once you get maps up and going lol).
  • Who were you closest to in your past?
    • In her childhood, Fitzie made friends with a few kids in her town (Retta Pith (she/her, half-orc) and Calder Ceslistan (they/them, human)). They’d hung out constantly, but fell distant once her mom started leaving more and more to bring back gold, which only caused Fitzie to almost exclusively talk about her for a time (she thought her mom was The Coolest Person Ever). Fitzie didn’t understand why they didn’t feel the same way.
  • Who do you consider family: by blood, bond, or oath?
    • She considers her bonds incredibly important, but does recognize when her word and oaths overshadow them.
  • Describe a moment or event that deeply shaped who you are now.
    • When she first went to the Collegeum Arcanum, she didn’t really have any sort of idea what she wanted to do. For at least the first few days, she had holed herself up in the library. While she didn’t exactly get kicked out, Thoqiohne did seem to eventually tire of her continued and overbearing presence and asked her to leave. She wandered the courtyard until she just sat in the grass and stared up at the evening sky. Professor Ilnudor found her there and just spent the next long while talking to her. It made her happy, and she sought out his classes to continue their discussion. He hasn’t gotten rid of her since.
