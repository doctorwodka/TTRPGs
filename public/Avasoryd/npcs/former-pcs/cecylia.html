Cecylia

SPOILERS

  • Where are they from?

    • She's a runaway from a small farming community called Bredani in south east Fallencall. She was never a farmer, and she was never that good in the arcane magics, so her and her collection of trinkets and doodads were never... appreciated, she thought.

  • Who were they to the community they were from?

    • The little kid who'd rather dig through junk than help out. She's sweet, yeah, but you have to drag her out of the house and physically put her in the field to get her to do anything and even then she obviously hates doing it. She's basically just a child, and now she's run away.
      • With Idris, she was just chatty little sidekick that followed him around and seemed to get on his nerves.

  • Who is their closest person? Where is that person right now?

    • Idris. He took her in and taught her what he knew for the tools of the trade after she tried to scam him on a piece of junk she found that she knew was worth nothing. He's... well. You know where.
    • Lilika Naas. She's a gnomish woman that Cyl met in her brief time in Welterglow, who had actually spent time in the Swashbuckler Society before taking a small break to be on land. She taught Cyl the bare beginnings of the bardic craft, which she took to like a fish in water.

  • What’s the most impactful moment in your characters life so far?

    • The first week of actually living on her own, she hadn't realized how hard it would be. She'd expected it to be hard, but not this hard. One night, she had set up camp, sat down, and just started crying. She missed home, she missed people so bad, and she was this close to packing things back up and heading there. But after she stopped sniffling, she shook her head and promised herself that she'd be okay. That she was going to make her own home somewhere, she'd make friends and her own family, and she'll be okay.

  • What’s your characters goal?

    • Helping those who have been affected by the wizards and protecting them from being hurt once again. Bringing Idris back.

  • What do you, as a player, want to get out of playing this character narratively?

    • Learning that she has to actually 'grow up' and realize that she doesn't have to take every little thing on herself. That she can be strong, but she doesn't have to be if there are those willing to help.

  • What do you, as a player, want to get out of this character from a mechanical/gameplay perspective?

    • :woozy: Other people not dying.
