Idris Lanthorn

  • Name: Idris Lanthorn
  • Age: 27
  • Pronouns: he/him
    Birthday: 4th of Temary, 610
SPOILERS

  • What would your character be doing if they were not in wizard experiment hell?

    • Same as he's always done: getting by by selling junk and useless trinkets to those who don't know any better.

  • Why won’t they go back to doing that as soon as they escape?

    • He's actually started to understand the junk he's collected can work well in a pinch. Sure, he could go back to selling junk, but why would he do that when he can actually get away with collecting stuff that actually works? Maybe he could actually find the break he's been looking for.

  • Where are they from?

    • Here and there. It's hard to get an answer from him about it sometimes, but those who really push could probably figure he's from some little settlement at the southernmost coast of Welterglow. Probably.

  • Who were they to the community they were from?

    • Local conman pedaling mostly fake "magic items" with a penchant for getting into trouble he really shouldn't be getting into. Mostly known for being able to sweet talk his way out of the worst of it.

  • Who is their closest person? Where is that person right now?

    • His apprentice, Cecylia. She's a young elven tiefling woman, around 19 y/o, with. He picked her up up not too long ago when she tried to scam him. They've become quite close, and he sees her quite a bit like family. She was out on a trip to a town in central Barrenbreak when he got taken; for all he knows, she thinks he's dead or has decided to leave her.
    • A distant cousin from Ravenyard, Ryker Maddox, who he's met and touched base with a couple of times. He's a good man, mostly, running a small company of bodyguards for hire. He's likely still there, if he hasn't taken up his next job.

  • What’s the most impactful moment in your characters life so far?

    • Beyond being captured and being experimented on by the wizards? The first time he got caught for selling someone a faulty piece of junk. He was only fourteen, voice cracks and all, and people trusted him. Heavily, in fact! He had that sort of trustworthy face. But he'll never forget the way that customer's face was distorted in rage as they chased him through the streets. He still chuckles thinking about it.

  • Why do the wizards think they can get away with experimenting on your character?

    • He's already such a willing test subject for the weird and the strange, though he didn't exactly expect his pedaling of totally legit "experimental" items would get him locked up in some wizards' test dungeon. Oops, that's his bad.

  • How long have you been captured by them?

    • Not long; probably six months at the best, a year at the worst. Time really blends together in there.

  • What kind of experiments have they been conducting on you?

    • Mutagens, magical items, and experimental talismans, mostly. The kind where you really don't want to be on the receiving end of; he's faced a good chunk magical backlash, but that's just allowed him to take the time to copy it all to memory and turn them back against those who captured him.

  • How is this reflected by your class/background/mechanical choices?

    • Thaumaturge collect the strange and turn it to their advantage. At level 2, I'll be taking the Talisman Esoterica feat to start being able to make talismans he remembers from the testing and using them to his advantage.

  • What’s your characters goal beyond simply escaping?

    • Expanding his occult knowledge to better understand why these things work the way they do. Additionally, he really wants to figure out what the end goal of all this experimentation is; he knows he's not the only one being experimented on, and frankly, he has some choice words and perhaps a punch to throw to whoever thought this all up.

  • What do you, as a player, want to get out of playing this character narratively?

    • I'm going back to my sleezey secrets era >:3 but really i wanted to create a character who could theoretically be idolized later in stories, but those who know him know he's kind of a shitty guy.

  • What do you, as a player, want to get out of this character from a mechanical/gameplay perspective?

    • i wanted to try something new! perhaps try a mix of mostly ranged martial with the ability to support + i wanted to mess around with familiars!

  • From a story perspective, why is your character the class they are? Why aren’t they any other class?

    • theoretically, he could have been a rogue, but his fascination with the occult was far greater than his wish to "legally take and sell" things.

  • How do your highest skills reflect your characters personality?

    • Deception being the highest for him... tracks the most with his conman tendencies. Thievery being his 2nd highest shows that he's not particularly the most trustworthy around potentially valuable items.

  • How does your character view themself? How do others view your character?

    • Reliable. A friend to those in need. A bit overly excessive at times, but who isn't? :)
    • Full of himself. Sleezy. A nice guy at the best of times, a selfish asshole at the worst. Does still try his best when it comes down to it, but gods, must it really be when it comes down to it?

  • Describe your characters moral or ethical beliefs. What would have to happen for them to go against their morals?

    • Theft and dishonesty are a natural part of life. If either happen to you, you shouldn't be upset about it.

    • The weak should be left alone; if you're picking on those who don't deserve it, you're worse than they'll ever be.

    • Make him feel bad; not emotionally, he's been called far too many names to count. Torture or, at the very least, threaten bodily harm when he can't get away and you've got your man.
