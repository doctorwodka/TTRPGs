What’s your characters goal?

  • Stopping Caius and preventing the spread of the Blackfrost and finding out why he was chosen by Nex Umbra.

What do you, as a player, want to get out of playing this character narratively?

  • I wanted to play a character who's able to settle into his own skin and create a name for himself rather than be in someone else's shadow. I really like the concept of the "twin" of such an evil guy is actively going against him despite unintentionally taking advantage of the fact that they share a face to do so. I also really want to learn more about what the mirror realm entails and why the FUCK they are sending these beasts to fight these reflections.

What do you, as a player, want to get out of this character from a mechanical/gameplay perspective?

  • Big big dammy from Solace, while still being able to cast spells. :3 I like the concept of kind of being the martial and the support caster all in one

From a story perspective, why is your character the class they are? Why aren’t they any other class?

  • Due to being "born" from a reflection, he wasn't able to defeat Solace and instead accepted this strange mirror beast as his summons.

How do your highest skills reflect your characters personality?

  • Arcana due to essentially being a wizard's apprentice for a little while, and has since tried to absorb as much information on the magical arts to try and understand what the FUCK is going on in the world magically
  • Athletics is from having to fucking BOOK IT whenever there had been the chance he was gonna get snatched up, and is now like. Okay. I guess I should actually try to not let them grab me like that again
  • Intimidation is due to the fear the Blackfrost puts into people, which he's using by, yknow, sharing his face

Describe your characters moral or ethical beliefs. What would have to happen for them to go against their morals?

  • The dead shouldn't be desecrated, especially if they're normal people caught in the crossfire of stronger adventurers. (Korkau has been REALLY pushing this for him. Very uncomfortable with their Dead Snatching.)

  • Lying & hiding the truth is okay, unless it actively puts others in immediate danger.

  • Murder under self-defense is fine, but if you're just killing to kill you're not really... going to be on his good side.

  • Magic is power. Those who have magic should be trying to help those who don't, and if you're not, you should have a good reason.

  • His friends or innocents would have to have be being threatened for him to go against his morals. However, he's quicker to cave if his own life is directly on the chopping block. He still wants to live. He wants to learn and grow.

  • Would Sin ever consider changing their face?

  • Honestly? Yes. If given the chance, he's have his face changed permanently so he doesn't have to face the comparisons to Caius ever again.

  • Who were they to the community when they traveled?

  • The odd guy with a giant tiger who, despite it, shrunk away from strangers at first. Eventually, he did open up to those who probed a bit, and he was more than willing to help out when needed. Never stuck around long, though, and did seem to be looking over his shoulder at any possible moment as if he was worried he was being watched.

  • Where did they travel before the church got them?

  • As far south as the northernmost tip of the Lunahelm Raiders. Without a map or worldly experience, it's kind of hard to get anywhere, really. Which, in hindsight, is probably for the best since he probably wouldn't have been a good fit there.

  • Tell me about your time as a wizards apprentice

  • He was overseen as he was taught how to work on cadavers by various Blackfrost workers. Elbows deep in rotting carcasses, only to later see them mindlessly walking to the Blackfrosts' orders. He was terrible at the actual raising part of necromancy, which was definitely... not ideal, but not the worst; at least he had some magical ability. He couldn't imagine what they would have had him do if he didn't have Solace.

  • What happened before you escaped?

  • He tried to learn about the world as much as he could. Half of the information went right over his head, but when he wasn't being forced into doing anything, he was sneaking books, or speaking with Solace, or probing the Blackfrost lackies for information.

  • How did you escape?

  • He took full advantage of the attack to hide Solace and sneak away in the heat of the moment. Stolen cloak and knife, the dark of night, and wandering blindly until he found a passage that was probably supposed to be under guard and he was... out. It shocked him, too. He spent all night running away until he hid in some poor family's barn to sleep. He couldn't shake the feeling that they let him escape but... he's yet to find proof of tht.

  • Did you get to know any members of the Blackfrost?

  • Very few Blackfrost members actually treated him more than some soulless husk for Lord Caius. Some were mean when he asked questions. Some were nice. The very, very few actually pitied him into speaking with him like a human being.

  • The closest he got to know was a halfling called Len. He's not sure if that was a nickname or his actual name, but Len treated him the most like a person. That doesn't mean he was treated well, but he was still treated like he had thoughts and opinions outside of Caius's own. Len was there because he was promised sanctuary from local law if he helped Lord Caius. Sinani managed to gleam that he did something quite bad but Len never let slip what exactly he did.

  • What’s the worst thing sin would do to survive?

  • Give up Solace, part of his soul, even it hurt him in the end (and by god it would hurt both of them). Go back to the Blackfrost, if things went truly to hell.

  • Would sin get rid of sol if given the chance?

  • Probably not unless he had very good reason to. Sinani's gotten pretty attached to Solace, even despite the "magically attached to his soul" part.
