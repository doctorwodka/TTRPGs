Personally Questions

  • What would your character be doing if they were not on this adventure?
    • Probably searching the coast for the suitable thing or information to prove that she's "all grown up" now. She's been mapping the weather, sketching down animals and plants she sees, in the hopes that she finds that "just right" proof that she's worthy of being a druid.
  • Why did they choose to take this boat? It’s not the first ship going out, it’s the first one headed south. It is not clear if there’s land in this direction.
    • It's a learning experience, and it didn't want to miss out on what could be nature that adapted to a land different than it's home. She wants to prove to her old druid summoner that she's capable of using this life given to her for good, and if not, she'll be another addition to the nature of this new land. This boat was chosen specifically because she'd been in the area and it has some connections to her swashbuckling friends.
  • Where are they from?
    • She was created from a small, flowering lotus pond not too far off the coast of Amberreach. She came into her own person, and not a familiar, after traveling with her druid summoner for a long while. She found she quite enjoyed the life on the sea, surrounded by the Swashbuckler Society.
  • Who were they to the community they were from?
    • The little chipper flower leshy that was a bit too nosey for what it's able to do. It's eager to learn, but it's more interested in the sea around the boat than the actual boat itself.
  • What has been the most impactful moment in your characters life so far?
    • Falling overboard after goofing around too much. As scary as diving into the water was, she found that floating on the waves, surrounded by a new aspect of nature she had yet to experience was comforting. She almost tried to dive back in when they fished her out!
  • What’s your characters goal?
    • Learning as much as it can about the world the crew is sailing into, tracking as much of this and gaining life experience as it can until it's certain that the people that watched it come into it's own would be proud of who it's become.
  • What is your characters relationship with the Swashbuckler Society and the Maronda Shipping Company?
    • It essentially "grew up" on one of the Swashbuckler Society's ships. Sunny's basically known sea-life longer than living on land.
  • From a narrative perspective, why is your character the class they are? Why aren’t they any other class?
    • Narratively, she's following in the footsteps of her original summoner.
  • How do your highest skills reflect your characters personality?
    • Medicine, Nature, and Survival all show that she is conscious of the environment around her and willing to protect it. It's happy to patch up her friends and those who have shown they're good to be around.
  • Describe your characters personal edicts and anathemas. Come up with a situation where they would to go against these beliefs.
  • Edicts:
    • Record Natural Phenomena: Observe and keep a record of natural phenomena, animal behavior, plant growth patterns, and weather changes.
    • Offer comfort in words - healing is more than physical.
    • Experience the physical world.
    • Take the Long Road: Take a long-term perspective in all aspects of life.
  • Anathemas:
    • Pollute the air, allow those who cause major air pollution or climate shifts to go unpunished.
    • Despoil natural places, consume more natural resources than you require to live comfortably, teach the Wildsong to non-druids.
    • Disturb Sacred Silence: Disrupt the natural silence of sacred groves or natural sanctuaries.
  • For her to go against these beliefs, she would have to literally come to terms that she's going to go against some of the things that were drilled into her as she learnt how to be a druid.
